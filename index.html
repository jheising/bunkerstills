The continuous still has revolutionized the alcohol industry, yet the cost and complexity of this amazing technology has rendered it nearly unobtainable to all but the largest distilleries— until now.

The Story

The Heising-330 prototype still in production use at the Whidbey Island Distillery

In the summer of 2010, Bev and Steve Heising and their two sons Jim and Kris opened the Whidbey Island Distillery (WID) on Whidbey Island, WA. Like many other craft distillers, WID began operations with a standard batch still and quickly discovered how much work was required to operate, maintain and clean its still— not to mention the experience required to make accurate and consistent cuts and the time and inefficiency of re-distillation to produce higher proof alcohols. After some months of struggling, WID realized that the workload and cost of labor to produce spirits on its batch still was simply unsustainable. There had to be a better way.

WID's Master Distiller, Steve Heising, knew that continuous distillation might be a solution, but the costs associated with continuous distillation were prohibitive to all but the largest distilleries. Yet, as a retired aerospace engineer and physicist, he thought that it should be theoretically possible to produce a reliable and cost-effective continuous still, and decided to give it a shot.

In early 2011, after a few months of research, design and building, WID had crafted an untested prototype still that (in theory) should produce clean alcohol in a continuous fashion. But would it work?

On 3/30/2011 at 3:30 pm, Steve connected a supply of wine to the still and began the heating process. Within minutes, a pure and crisp neutral grape spirit emerged through the parrot. While the date and time of the first run was pure coincidence, it seemed only fitting to dedicate the moment when a revolution in distillation began for WID, and quite possibly the rest of the craft distillation industry at large. The Heising-330 was born.

Since that date, the Heising-330 has been tested, refined and updated in the real-world with thousands of gallons of alcohol, and has produced award-winning and top-selling spirits for a loyal legion of fans.
